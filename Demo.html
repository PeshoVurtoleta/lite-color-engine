The Perceptual Proof
A · B Comparison

Interpolating colors in sRGB drags every gradient through a muddy mid-band — the so-called "dead zone" where lightness collapses and hue drifts. OKLCH is perceptually uniform: it interpolates the way the eye sees. Same two endpoints, different spaces. Look closely at the middle.

Observed The sRGB blend dips through a desaturated grey at the midpoint — the eye reads the gradient as having a dirty patch. The OKLCH blend holds chroma and walks the hue along the shortest perceptual arc. This is the entire reason OKLCH exists — and the entire reason this library exists.
