colour is a 256-cell LUT

Every primitive's colour is one gather from a 256-entry LUT, baked by lite-color-lerp and packed by lite-color-engine. Moving a slider re-bakes 256 cells — never a million. That is the whole trick, and it is why the sliders don't lag at 1M points.

LUT bake is the entire cost of a slider move: 256 OKLCH→sRGB conversions. Converting every primitive instead would be – — 57× over the frame budget at 1M.

Above ~500K the limit is uploading positions (30 MB/frame at 1M — 1.8 GB/s to hold 60), not colour. That is the point: colour never becomes the bottleneck, at any count. Drop to 100K for a locked 60.

rAF Δ is the honest number. ~8.3 ms means the browser is giving you 120 Hz and any low fps is this page's fault. ~16.7 or ~33 ms means the browser is throttling and nothing in this file can fix it. draws should equal fps — if it is lower, frames are being skipped.
