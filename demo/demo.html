The Perceptual Proof
A · B Comparison

Interpolating colors in sRGB drags every gradient through a muddy mid-band — the so-called "dead zone" where lightness collapses and hue drifts. OKLCH is perceptually uniform: it interpolates the way the eye sees. Same two endpoints, different spaces. Look closely at the middle — the deltaEOK readout quantifies exactly how far off the sRGB midpoint drifts.

The Gamut Boundary
/gamut · CSS Color 4 MINDE

Saturated OKLCH colors that fall outside the sRGB triangle need to be pulled back in. The old way — clamp each channel to [0, 1] — introduces a visible hue shift near the boundary. MINDE reduces chroma along a line of constant lightness and hue, using a JND-thresholded bisection. Same L, same H, less C. Watch what happens along the edge.

Observed Every colored pixel is inside the sRGB gamut in both panels — the difference is where the excess chroma went. Hard-clamp produces flat-topped regions where different channels pin at 1.0 independently, creating false hue edges. MINDE trades chroma for hue-and-lightness fidelity, along the perceptually correct axis.
05
The Recolor
/remap · SoA nearest-palette

Take any raster, lock a palette, replace every pixel with its nearest palette color by ΔE-OK — in OKLab, at native memory speed. Set preserveLightness and the source image's own shading survives the recolor. This is the specific thing generative AI color tools structurally cannot do: deterministic, hue-controlled recoloring at 60 fps.
