Accuracy Tiers

One vivid OKLCH sweep packed three ways. FAST swaps a sqrt for the transfer (~10/255 drift). ACCURATE uses the true transfer with a hard clamp. GAMUT runs MINDE chroma reduction from the /gamut subpath to hold hue near the boundary.

P3 is v1.5's packOklchBufferToUint32P3IntoN writing into a display-p3 canvas -- one batch call for the whole row. The colorSpace is fixed at getContext() time, which is why this is its own cell rather than a toggle.

Blue-Noise Dither (v1.5)

A shallow lightness ramp -- the case 8-bit output cannot represent. Left is packOklchBufferToUint32IntoN: the encoder rounds, and the ramp collapses into hard stair-steps. Right is packOklchBufferToUint32IntoNDithered, walking the shared 64x64 tile from getBlueNoise64(). Same bytes available, same buffer, no allocations -- the error is just scattered instead of accumulated.

Push base L into the highlights (0.85+) and shrink delta L. That is the regime the v1.5.0 release candidate got wrong.

Cyclic LUT Bake (v1.5)

The same hue-wheel stops baked twice and mapped around a ring. Open mode spreads the stops over [0, 1] and stops at the last one -- so where the ring closes, the LUT jumps straight back to stop 0. Closed mode ({ closed: true }) adds the wrap segment last-to-first and spaces samples at i / resolution, so there is no duplicated endpoint and no seam.

The vivid stops are pulled into sRGB first with one gamutMapToSrgbBufferN call over the whole keyframe buffer.

Wheel

The ease is t + 0.35 -- deliberate overshoot past 1. Open mode clamps it (the tail flattens into a solid arc); closed mode period-wraps it, which just rotates the wheel.
